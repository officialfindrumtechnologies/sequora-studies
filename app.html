    Study sequence

    Where to start — the exact order

    Which subject gets which block

    Block 1 (sharpest hours, 7:30am) = Maths A — least crammable, gets your best brain every day.
    Block 2 (10:00) = Accounting — pure dependency chain, front-loaded.
    Block 3 (12:15) = Economics / Business — alternate daily. Evaluation marks decide A*.
    Block 4 (lighter, 3:00) + weekends = English B / Bangla / past papers — skill subjects live here.
    Principle: front-load what you can't cram later. Maths & Accounting can't be crammed in September.

    Which chapter first within a subject

    Follow the topic order top-to-bottom in each subject's tracker — they're already sequenced so prerequisites come before what needs them. Don't jump ahead; finish a topic to Exam-ready before the next. Drop in an easy "island" topic when you want a confidence win.

    The daily loop per topic

    1. Learn — NotebookLM with the spec loaded (~25 min)
    2. Note in your own words (~5 min)
    3. Drill past-paper questions on that topic (~30 min)
    4. Quiz yourself unaided in Gemini (~10 min)
    5. Mark the tracker
    A topic is only Exam-ready when you score well unaided against the mark scheme.
